Cybercrime Analysis: A Malaysian and Global Perspective

DID YOU KNOW A CYBERATTACK CAN HAPPEN EVERY 39 SECONDS?

Cybercrime has become a pervasive threat in today's digital landscape, impacting individuals, businesses, and governments alike. With the rapid advancement of technology and the increasing reliance on the internet for communication, commerce, and information sharing, cybercriminals have found new opportunities to exploit vulnerabilities. Cybercrime encompasses a wide range of illegal activities, including identity theft, data breaches, financial fraud, and cyberbullying, and others. Therefore, this visualization will explore the factors and implications of cybercrime in Malaysia and globally.

Choropleth Map: National Cybersecurity Index

The visualization begins by demonstrating the National Cybersecurity Index for 2023, offering insights into the ability of various countries to prevent and manage cyber incidents. Belgium holds the highest index score of 94.81%, indicating its strong capability in handling cyber incidents. Malaysia, with a score of 79.22%, reflects a moderately high capability. The United States of America, with a score of 64.94% and China at 51.95%, demonstrates a relatively moderate capacity to manage cybersecurity risks, despite the advancement of technology in both country. Myanmmar with the score of 10.39% shows a very low capacity to manage cybersecurity risks.

Waffle Chart: Average Weekly Attack Over Region

Moving on to the next chart,
Average weekly attack rate by region reveals that Africa has the highest percentage at 31%, indicating poor cybersecurity prevention across the continent. This is followed by the Asia-Pacific (APAC) region with 27%, and Latin America showing a moderate level at 16% attacks. Regions like Europe and North America have the lowest percentages, both at 13% reflecting stronger cybersecurity defenses.

Bar Chart: Malaysia Botnet Drones and Malware Infections Over Years

Next, to further explore the cyber incidents in Malaysia, focusing on Botnet Drones and Malware Infections from 2013 to 2023. The trend started to rise in 2014 and then remained relatively consistent in subsequent years. However, during the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020, there was a significant increase, with infections reaching nearly 5.5 million. By 2023, the number hit a peak of 7.45 million, marking the highest level recorded during this period.

Stacked Area Chart: Most Reported Cyber Incidents by Threat Type in Malaysia

This visualization highlights the Cyber Incidents by Threat Type in Malaysia from 2013 to 2023.
The highest number of incidents occurred in 2014, with approximately 13,000 cases.
Fraud consistently accounts for the largest proportion of incidents, indicating that financial-related crimes are highly targeted by cybercriminals.
Vulnerability Reports show the lowest proportion, suggesting that either fewer incidents are being reported through official channels or vulnerabilities are underreported.

Bump Chart: Most Targeted Industries of Cyber Incidents

To further explore, the visualization provide a clear ranking of the most targeted industries for cybercrime from 2017 to 2023. Financial Sector consistently been a top target for cyber incidents, ranking #1 in 2023 with the highest score of 24.9. Social Media shows a sharp rise moving from a lower position to #2 at 2023 due to the increasing usage of social platforms making it attractive to attackers. Payment systems, originally ranked first in 2017 and 2018, have seen a notable drop, falling to #6 place by 2023 indicate improvement security measures in the payment has been made over the years.

Bidirectional Bar Chart: Most Used Social Media Platform

Social Media ranks as the second most targeted sector by cybercriminals, this chart focus on the top 15 most used social media platforms, comparing two key metrics: Most Active Users and Monthly Organic Traffic.
Facebook dominates, leading both metrics with 3,000 million active users and 10,000 million in organic traffic. YouTube follows closely with substantial user activity and traffic. Platforms like Instagram, WhatsApp, and TikTok shows high organic traffic but comparatively fewer active users.
